Why people don't want to code (causes)

  • The majority of people who code, find it boring.
  • When troubleshooting occurs, it may be difficult.
  • Also, some people just can't code- it's not for them.

Why people should want to code (effects)

  • Not learning to code denies valuable skills, such as critical thinking.
  • Coding provides many useful utilities in the real world, and can give you shortcuts.
  • Giving up is never fun, and you may be sad if you quit.

What's wrong with not wanting to code (history/seriousness)

  • Many websites teach how to code, yet are very difficult/expensive.
  • Some websites don't teach you some of the most essential skills in coding.
  • Even though websites such as code.org have taught many, there can't be too many programmers. Go to Why Code? for a more detailed explaination.
